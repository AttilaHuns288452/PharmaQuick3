Lansoprazole 15mg Capsules

Digestive Health

₱120.00 ₱135.00

Lansoprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) that reduces stomach acid production to relieve symptoms of acid-related conditions including heartburn, acid reflux, and stomach ulcers.

Description

Lansoprazole belongs to a class of medications called proton pump inhibitors (PPIs). It works by decreasing the amount of acid produced in the stomach, which helps to relieve symptoms of various acid-related conditions. This medication is used to treat conditions where reducing stomach acid is needed, such as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), ulcers, and Zollinger-Ellison syndrome.

Active Ingredient

Each delayed-release capsule contains 15mg of Lansoprazole.

Uses

  • Treatment of heartburn and other symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Short-term treatment of active duodenal ulcers
  • Short-term treatment of active benign gastric ulcer
  • Healing of erosive esophagitis
  • Reduction of risk of NSAID-associated gastric ulcers
  • Treatment of pathological hypersecretory conditions, including Zollinger-Ellison syndrome

How It Works

Lansoprazole works by blocking the enzyme in the wall of the stomach that produces acid. By reducing acid production, it:

  • Decreases symptoms of heartburn and acid reflux
  • Allows damaged tissue in the esophagus, stomach, or duodenum to heal
  • Prevents further damage to these tissues
  • Provides longer-lasting acid reduction compared to antacids and H2 blockers

Dosage

For adults:

  • Heartburn/GERD: 15mg once daily for up to 8 weeks
  • Duodenal Ulcers: 15mg once daily for 4 weeks
  • Gastric Ulcers: 30mg once daily for up to 8 weeks
  • Erosive Esophagitis: 30mg once daily for up to 8 weeks

Important dosing instructions:

  • Take before meals (preferably before breakfast)
  • Swallow capsules whole; do not crush or chew
  • If you have difficulty swallowing capsules, they can be opened and the contents sprinkled on a tablespoon of applesauce, then swallowed immediately
  • Do not store the mixture for future use

Precautions

Before taking Lansoprazole, tell your doctor if you have:

  • Liver disease
  • Low magnesium levels
  • Osteoporosis or risk factors for bone fractures
  • Phenylketonuria (PKU) - some formulations may contain phenylalanine
  • Planned endoscopic procedure with sodium picosulfate
  • History of vitamin B12 deficiency

Drug Interactions: Lansoprazole may interact with:

  • Atazanavir and nelfinavir (HIV medications)
  • Warfarin (blood thinner)
  • Methotrexate
  • Digoxin
  • Iron supplements
  • Clopidogrel
  • Tacrolimus
  • Sucralfate
  • St. John's Wort

Possible Side Effects

Common side effects:

  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Stomach pain
  • Constipation

Less common but serious side effects:

  • Severe diarrhea with watery stools, stomach pain, and fever
  • Bone fractures (with long-term use)
  • Low magnesium levels (symptoms include muscle spasms, irregular heartbeat, seizures)
  • Vitamin B12 deficiency (with long-term use)
  • Kidney problems
  • Severe skin reactions
  • Lupus-like syndrome (joint pain, skin rash on cheeks or arms)

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:

  • Signs of an allergic reaction (hives, difficulty breathing, swelling of face, lips, tongue, or throat)
  • Severe stomach pain
  • Seizures
  • Kidney problems (urinating less than usual, blood in urine, swelling, rapid weight gain)
  • Symptoms of low magnesium (dizziness, confusion, fast or irregular heart rate, jitteriness, muscle cramps)

Storage

  • Store at room temperature (20-25°C or 68-77°F)
  • Keep away from moisture and heat
  • Keep the container tightly closed
  • Keep out of reach of children

Important Information

  • Lansoprazole is intended for short-term use (usually 4-8 weeks) unless otherwise directed by a healthcare provider
  • Long-term use may increase the risk of certain side effects
  • If symptoms persist or worsen after 14 days of treatment, consult a healthcare provider
  • This medication may mask symptoms of stomach cancer; consult a doctor if you have warning signs like unexplained weight loss or recurring vomiting
  • Proton pump inhibitors may increase the risk of certain infections in the digestive tract
