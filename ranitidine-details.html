Ranitidine 150mg Tablets

Digestive Health

₱180.00 ₱210.00

Ranitidine is an H2 (histamine-2) blocker that reduces the amount of acid produced in the stomach. It provides effective relief from heartburn, acid indigestion, and sour stomach, and can help prevent these symptoms when taken before meals.

Description

Ranitidine belongs to a class of medications called H2 (histamine-2) blockers. It works by reducing the amount of acid your stomach produces by blocking histamine, a natural substance in the body that stimulates acid secretion in the stomach.

Our Ranitidine 150mg tablets provide effective relief from acid-related discomfort and can be used to treat and prevent various conditions caused by excess stomach acid, including heartburn, acid indigestion, sour stomach, and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). It can also help heal and prevent ulcers in the stomach and intestines.

Note: In some countries, ranitidine products have been withdrawn from the market due to concerns about a contaminant called N-nitrosodimethylamine (NDMA). Please consult with your healthcare provider or pharmacist about current recommendations in your area.

Active Ingredient

Each tablet contains 150mg of Ranitidine (as ranitidine hydrochloride).

Uses

  • Relieves and prevents heartburn associated with acid indigestion and sour stomach
  • Treats and prevents symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Helps heal and prevent duodenal and gastric ulcers
  • Treats conditions in which the stomach produces too much acid, such as Zollinger-Ellison syndrome
  • Prevents heartburn when taken 30-60 minutes before meals or activities that may cause heartburn
  • May be used as part of a treatment regimen for H. pylori infection (under medical supervision)

How It Works

Ranitidine works by:

  • Blocking histamine H2 receptors on the cells in the stomach lining
  • Reducing the production of stomach acid by inhibiting histamine's action
  • Decreasing both the volume and concentration of acid that the stomach produces
  • Providing relief from acid-related symptoms for up to 12 hours
  • Creating a less acidic environment that allows damaged tissue in the digestive tract to heal

Dosage

For adults and children 12 years and older:

  • To relieve symptoms: Take 1 tablet (150mg) with a glass of water as needed, up to twice daily
  • To prevent symptoms: Take 1 tablet (150mg) 30-60 minutes before eating food or drinking beverages that cause heartburn
  • Do not take more than 2 tablets (300mg) in 24 hours
  • Swallow tablet whole - do not chew, crush, or dissolve
  • Can be taken with or without food
  • Do not use for more than 14 days continuously unless directed by a doctor

Children under 12 years:

  • Not recommended for use in children without medical supervision

Precautions

Do not use if you:

  • Are allergic to ranitidine or other H2 blockers (such as cimetidine, famotidine, or nizatidine)
  • Have trouble swallowing or persistent abdominal pain
  • Have bloody or black stools
  • Are vomiting blood or material that looks like coffee grounds

Ask a doctor before use if you have:

  • Had heartburn for more than 3 months
  • Heartburn with lightheadedness, sweating, or dizziness
  • Chest or shoulder pain with shortness of breath, sweating, pain spreading to arms, neck or shoulders, or lightheadedness
  • Frequent chest pain
  • Frequent wheezing, particularly with heartburn
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Stomach pain
  • Kidney or liver disease

Ask a doctor or pharmacist before use if you are:

  • Taking other medications, as ranitidine may interact with certain drugs
  • Taking a prescription medication for:
    • Anticoagulation (blood thinning)
    • Anti-seizure drugs
    • Diabetes
    • Gout
    • Arthritis
    • Heart problems
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding

Stop use and ask a doctor if:

  • Your heartburn continues or worsens
  • You need to take this product for more than 14 days
  • You develop new or unexpected symptoms
  • You experience confusion, depression, or hallucinations
  • You develop yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice)
  • You develop unusual bleeding or bruising

Possible Side Effects

When taken as directed, ranitidine is generally well-tolerated. However, possible side effects may include:

Common side effects:

  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Constipation or diarrhea
  • Nausea
  • Stomach discomfort

Less common side effects:

  • Drowsiness
  • Fatigue
  • Insomnia
  • Decreased sex drive
  • Rash or itching
  • Muscle or joint pain

Rare but serious side effects (seek immediate medical attention):

  • Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling, severe dizziness, trouble breathing)
  • Confusion, agitation, depression, or hallucinations
  • Blurred vision
  • Unusual bleeding or bruising
  • Severe stomach pain
  • Yellowing of the skin or eyes (jaundice)
  • Dark urine
  • Fast, slow, or irregular heartbeat
  • Hair loss
  • Severe skin reactions

Storage

  • Store at room temperature (20-25°C or 68-77°F)
  • Keep away from moisture and heat
  • Keep the container tightly closed
  • Keep out of reach of children

Drug Interactions

Ranitidine may interact with several medications. Always consult with a healthcare professional before taking ranitidine if you are taking any of the following:

  • Blood thinners (such as warfarin)
  • Medications that require stomach acid for absorption (such as ketoconazole, itraconazole, atazanavir)
  • Procainamide (heart rhythm medication)
  • Triazolam (sedative)
  • Glipizide (diabetes medication)
  • Medications that are affected by changes in stomach pH
  • Medications that are processed by the liver

Lifestyle and Diet Tips for Acid Reflux

In addition to taking Ranitidine as directed, these lifestyle changes may help manage acid reflux symptoms:

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals
  • Avoid lying down for 2-3 hours after eating
  • Elevate the head of your bed 6-8 inches
  • Avoid trigger foods such as:
    • Spicy, fatty, or fried foods
    • Citrus fruits and tomatoes
    • Chocolate and mint
    • Onions and garlic
    • Carbonated beverages
    • Coffee and alcohol
  • Quit smoking
  • Avoid tight-fitting clothing around the waist
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques

When to Seek Medical Attention

While ranitidine is generally safe when used as directed, certain situations require prompt medical attention:

  • If symptoms persist for more than 14 days or worsen
  • If you experience severe or persistent abdominal pain
  • If you have difficulty swallowing
  • If you vomit blood or have black/tarry stools
  • If you experience chest pain with shortness of breath, sweating, or pain spreading to the jaw, neck, or arm
  • If you experience any severe side effects after taking ranitidine

Important Information

Ranitidine provides relief from acid-related symptoms but does not immediately relieve heartburn. It typically begins working within an hour and provides relief for up to 12 hours. For immediate relief of heartburn, antacids may be more appropriate.

Long-term or high-dose use of acid reducers, including ranitidine, may increase the risk of certain conditions, including bone fractures, vitamin B12 deficiency, and certain infections. Discuss with your healthcare provider if you need to use this medication for an extended period.
