Paracetamol 500mg Tablets

Pain Relief & Fever Reducer

₱85.00 ₱100.00

Paracetamol (also known as Acetaminophen) is a widely used over-the-counter pain reliever and fever reducer. These 500mg tablets provide effective relief from mild to moderate pain and help reduce high temperature.

Description

Paracetamol is one of the most commonly used medications worldwide for pain management and fever reduction. It works by affecting chemicals in the body that cause pain and fever. Unlike NSAIDs (such as ibuprofen or aspirin), paracetamol doesn't reduce inflammation significantly but has fewer gastrointestinal side effects, making it suitable for a wider range of people.

Our Paracetamol 500mg tablets are easy to swallow and provide fast-acting relief for various types of pain, including headaches, toothaches, backaches, menstrual pain, muscle aches, and pain associated with colds and flu. They are also effective at reducing fever.

Active Ingredient

Each tablet contains 500mg of Paracetamol (Acetaminophen).

Uses

  • Temporarily relieves mild to moderate pain due to:
    • Headaches and migraines
    • Toothache
    • Backache
    • Menstrual cramps
    • Muscular aches
    • Minor arthritis pain
    • Cold and flu symptoms
    • Sore throat
  • Reduces fever

How It Works

Paracetamol works by:

  • Inhibiting the production of prostaglandins in the central nervous system (brain and spinal cord)
  • Affecting the hypothalamus (the part of the brain that regulates temperature) to help reduce fever
  • Blocking pain signals to the brain
  • Unlike NSAIDs, paracetamol has minimal effect on prostaglandins in peripheral tissues, which is why it doesn't reduce inflammation significantly

Dosage

Adults and children 12 years and over:

  • 1-2 tablets (500-1000mg) every 4-6 hours as needed
  • Do not take more than 8 tablets (4000mg) in 24 hours
  • Do not take for more than 10 days for pain (5 days for fever) unless directed by a doctor
  • Take with a full glass of water

Children 6-11 years:

  • ½ tablet (250mg) every 4-6 hours as needed
  • Do not give more than 4 doses (1000mg) in 24 hours
  • Do not give for more than 5 days unless directed by a doctor

Children under 6 years:

  • Not recommended without medical supervision
  • Use children's paracetamol formulations specifically designed for this age group

Precautions

Do not use if you:

  • Are allergic to paracetamol or any ingredients in this product
  • Have severe liver disease
  • Consume three or more alcoholic drinks every day (increased risk of liver damage)

Ask a doctor before use if you have:

  • Liver disease
  • Kidney disease
  • Phenylketonuria (PKU) - some formulations may contain aspartame
  • A history of alcohol abuse
  • A severe infection, are severely malnourished, or are severely dehydrated

Ask a doctor or pharmacist before use if you are:

  • Taking the blood-thinning drug warfarin
  • Taking other medications containing paracetamol (to avoid overdose)
  • Taking other medications that might affect liver function
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding

Stop use and ask a doctor if:

  • Pain gets worse or lasts for more than 10 days
  • Fever gets worse or lasts for more than 3 days
  • Redness or swelling is present
  • New symptoms occur
  • You experience signs of liver problems (such as dark urine, pale stools, yellowing of skin/eyes, stomach pain, unusual tiredness)

Possible Side Effects

When taken as directed, paracetamol rarely causes side effects. However, possible side effects may include:

Rare side effects:

  • Allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling)
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Stomach pain

Very rare side effects:

  • Blood disorders (unusual bleeding or bruising)
  • Skin reactions (severe rash, peeling)

Overdose warning:

Taking more than the recommended dose can cause liver damage, which can be serious and potentially fatal. Symptoms of overdose may include:

  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Stomach pain
  • Sweating
  • Confusion
  • Yellowing of the skin or whites of the eyes (jaundice)

If you suspect an overdose, seek medical attention immediately, even if there are no symptoms, as liver damage may not be immediately apparent.

Storage

  • Store at room temperature (20-25°C or 68-77°F)
  • Keep away from moisture and heat
  • Keep the container tightly closed
  • Keep out of reach of children

Important Information

  • Alcohol warning: If you consume 3 or more alcoholic drinks every day, ask your doctor whether you should take paracetamol or other pain relievers/fever reducers. Paracetamol may cause liver damage.
  • Avoid multiple products: Do not use with other products containing paracetamol, as this may lead to overdose.
  • Proper dosing: Always follow the recommended dosage. Taking more than directed can cause liver damage.
  • Medical attention: If pain or fever persists or worsens, consult a healthcare professional.

When to Seek Medical Attention

While paracetamol is generally safe when used as directed, certain situations require prompt medical attention:

  • If you suspect you've taken too much paracetamol (even if you feel well)
  • If fever persists for more than 3 days
  • If pain persists for more than 10 days
  • If you experience severe or unusual symptoms while taking paracetamol
  • If you notice signs of an allergic reaction (rash, itching, swelling, severe dizziness, difficulty breathing)
  • If you notice signs of liver problems (yellowing of skin/eyes, dark urine, pale stools, unusual tiredness, stomach pain)
